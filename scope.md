# Scope

The model is a **single tube swept along one logarithmic spiral around one coiling axis**
(Raup W/D/T + ornament/pigment/color). This defines what it can and cannot represent.

## In scope (covered)

- **Gastropods** — snails, cones, augers, turbans, limpets. Full coverage; this is the core case.
- **Bivalve valves** — a *single* valve as a high-W, n<1 spiral (radial ribs ≈ axial ribs,
  growth lines ≈ spiral cords).
- **Tusk shells (Scaphopoda)** — gently curved tapering tube as a low-W, n<1 spiral.
- **Regular-coiled worm tubes** — e.g. *Spirorbis* (small planispiral).

## Out of scope (not covered)

- **Bivalve pairing** — two mirror-image valves, the hinge, and gape/closure. We model one valve,
  not the hinged pair.
- **Chitons (Polyplacophora)** — 8 serial articulated plates + girdle. A segmented plan, not a spiral.
- **Echinoids (sea urchins, sand dollars)** — pentaradial (5-fold) disks/domes. Only the gross
  silhouette is approximable; the radial plate/petal architecture is not.
- **Irregular annelid tubes** — meandering, cemented tubes (e.g. *Serpula*) follow no logarithmic
  spiral and are not parameterizable.

## Note

Coverage spans **accretionary, single-aperture, spirally-grown shells**. Body plans built on
**paired**, **serial-segmented**, or **radially-symmetric** growth are intentionally excluded to
keep the architecture simple.
